2. MARKING INSTRUCTIONS

  1. After Co-ordination Meeting, you will mark under the supervision of your Chief Examiner/Team Leader who will scrutinize the worked scripts marked by you to ensure that you are marking in conformity with the agreed scheme of marking.
  2. Examiners are required to mark in accordance with the marking scheme and the instructions given by the Chief Examiner/Team Leader.
  3. Examiners must bear in mind the need for accuracy in the addition and subtraction of marks, as well as in the assessment of candidates' work. It is also the personal responsibility of each examiner to ensure that the marks entered on the scripts (and eventually on the Attendance/Mark Sheets) are accurate.
  4. The following instructions must be carried out:
      {% if show_red_marking_pen_instruction %}
    1. Unless otherwise specified, all {{ examiner_role_title }}s must use only a RED BALL POINT PEN for marking.
      2. {% endif %} {% if show_green_vetting_pen_instruction %}
    2. {{ examiner_role_title }}s must use GREEN BALL POINT PEN for vetting.
      3. {% else %}
    3. Chief Examiners/Team Leader must use GREEN BALL POINT PEN for vetting.
      4. {% endif %}
    4. The mark given, (even if it is '0') for each answer must be written clearly and ringed thus (40) in the left-hand margin immediately before the answer to which it refers. The mark for the answer must be CHECKED by carrying out the addition of marks for each point or part of the answer in the reverse order. If the mark is proved to be correct, the ringed total must be TICKED (√).
    5. Care must be taken to ENSURE THAT NO WORK IS OVERLOOKED. Candidates sometimes leave one or more pages blank in the middle of their work, and it is therefore necessary for the examiner to glance at every page of the answer booklet. The number of questions which the candidate has answered should be compared with the number entered on the front cover of the answer booklet. The total mark for the paper should appear at the right-hand edge of the last sheet of the answer book and it is a useful check for which marks are given in the grid. It is advisable to cross out every blank page of the answer booklet.

No fractional marks should appear in the total score for the paper; if the total mark scored by a candidate involves a fraction, this must be rounded to the nearest integer. Marks for answers to sub-questions or whole question must retain their fractions.

Every examiner will be required to mark a minimum of twenty (20) scripts at the end of every day.
